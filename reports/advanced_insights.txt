
## Advanced Analytics - 5 Key Insights

Insight 1 - Highest VaR Funds:
Small Cap and Sectoral funds consistently show the worst
VaR (below -2.5%), meaning on any bad day investors could
lose more than 2.5% of their investment.
Moderate risk investors should avoid these schemes.
Chart: reports/charts/16_var_cvar.png

Insight 2 - Investor Cohort Behaviour:
Investors who started SIPs in 2022-2023 show higher
average SIP amounts compared to earlier cohorts,
indicating growing financial awareness and higher
disposable income among newer investors.
Chart: reports/charts/18_cohort_analysis.png

Insight 3 - SIP Continuity Rate:
Approximately 70%+ investors maintain regular SIP gaps
under 35 days, showing strong SIP discipline.
At-risk investors with irregular SIPs represent a key
retention opportunity for fund houses.
Chart: reports/charts/19_sip_continuity.png

Insight 4 - Rolling Sharpe Patterns:
Rolling 90-day Sharpe ratios reveal that most funds
delivered Sharpe > 1 during 2023 bull run but dropped
significantly during 2024 market corrections,
highlighting the importance of timing in fund selection.
Chart: reports/charts/17_rolling_sharpe.png

Insight 5 - Sector Concentration Risk:
Funds with high HHI scores (>0.25) are heavily
concentrated in Financial Services and IT sectors.
While this can amplify returns in bull markets,
it significantly increases downside risk during
sector-specific corrections.
Chart: reports/charts/20_hhi_concentration.png
